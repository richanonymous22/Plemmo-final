The problem

Most businesses are on
out-of-date tariffs.

When a business energy contract ends, suppliers often roll customers onto expensive default tariffs. Many business owners are paying significantly more than they need to — without realising it.

Rolling tariffs cost more

When fixed contracts expire without a renewal, suppliers move you onto standard variable tariffs — often significantly higher.

Complex contracts

Business energy contracts can be harder to understand than personal ones — with different rates, capacity charges and standing charges.

No one's reviewing your bill

Unlike domestic customers, most businesses don't have anyone proactively reviewing their energy spend. We can be that person.

Common questions

Will switching supplier interrupt my energy?

No. Switching business energy is a contractual process — your gas or electricity supply is never physically interrupted. The pipes and cables remain the same; only the billing company changes.

Is Plemmo an energy supplier?

No. Plemmo is an introducer. We work with a panel of trusted UK energy suppliers and introduce businesses to suitable options. We may receive commission from suppliers for these introductions.

How long does it take to switch?

Switch timescales vary depending on the supplier and your meter type, but most business energy switches complete within 3–6 weeks of signing. We'll keep you updated throughout.

Can you help multi-site businesses?

Yes. If you operate multiple locations, we can review energy across all sites and look for consolidated or site-by-site options as appropriate.

What if I'm in an existing contract?

We can review your current contract end date and start arranging a new deal ahead of renewal, so you don't roll onto a default tariff. We can also advise on exit fees where applicable.
